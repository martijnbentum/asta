Transcription protocol

Goal

Annotating the quality of the alignment between a short transcription and the audio.

Background

The Meertens institute recorded speech in different dialects from all over the Netherlands. Some of these recording were transcribed in a semi-phonemic fashion. The transcriptions use standard Dutch spelling, but deviate to some extent to reflect the pronounciation of a word in a given the dialect. The paper documents containing the transcriptions were scanned and OCRed to create text files. These transcriptions were not aligned with the audio recordings.

To align the manual transcriptions, we created automatic transcriptions of the audio recordings with a Dutch Wav2vec automatic speech recognition (ASR) system trained on the Spoken Dutch Corpus (i.e. utilizing standard Dutch spelling). In a second step this ASR transcription was aligned with the manual transcriptions. The goal of the current annotations is to rate the quality of the alignment between the manual transcription and the audio.

Selecting materials to annotate

You can annotate by province or area (streek). You can use different settings to select materials. The materials are organized per recording. A recording is a single audio file. Each transcribed sentence/phrase is considered a transcription.

exclude recordings Exclude recordings based on whether you or anyone annotated the recording (this does not necessarily mean all transcriptions are annotated

exclude transcription Exclude transcriptions based on whether you or anyone annotated the recording. This can be usefull if you want to annotate more materials of a province you annotated before or if you want to make sure you are only annotating materials not annotated before.

minmum match This a percentage and selects only to transcriptions that that have a percentage match on the character of that number or higher between the manual and automatic (wav2vec) version of the transcription. If you set this very low you might get a lot transcriptions with bad alignment If you set it to high you might only get transcriptions that are ok. The setting is good if you a bit of both so we can figure out what number to use if we do not have manual annotations.

percentage material Sets the percentage of transcriptions per recordings to be annotated. To distribute the annotation efforts over different recordings it can be good not to annotate all transcriptions of all recordings. By default a minimum of 30 and a maximum of 100 transcriptions are selected per recording.

resume On the homepage (click Asta in the upper left corner). you choose to resume annotation. This will take you to the last transcription you were working on last time.

Annotation terms

bad no or very minimal overlap between audio and transcription

good transcription matches the audio

start match the start of the transcription matches the audio, but the end of the transcription is not present in the audio. Copy paste the matching part of the transcription to the corrected transcription box

middle match the middle of the transcription matches the audio, but the start and end of the transcription is not present in the audio. Copy paste the matching part of the transcription to the corrected transcription box

middle mismatch the start and end of the transcription match the audio, but the middle of the transcription is not present in the audio. Copy paste the matching part of the transcription to the corrected transcription box

end match the end of the transcription matches the audio, but the start of the transcription is not present in the audio. Copy paste the matching part of the transcription to the corrected transcription box

Tips & Tricks

to speed up annotation you can use keys to navigate and annotate the materials, below a list of them. The shortcut keys for the annotation buttons are bolded.

spacebar start the audio fragment. You can also click the play icon above the transcription

p (previous) go to the previous transcription. Only works if there is one. You can also click the left arrow in the upper right corner.

n (next) go to the next transcription. Only works if this transcription is annotated. you can also click the right arrow in the upper right corner.

b annotate with label 'bad'

s annotate with label 'start match'

m annotate with label 'middle match'

i annotate with label 'middle mismatch'

e annotate with label 'end match'

g annotate with label 'good'
